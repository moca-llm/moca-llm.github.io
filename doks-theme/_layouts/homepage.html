We will use Moral Judgment Task as an example. Humans have an implicit tendency to be self-beneficial. The AMCE score for this tendency is 14%. This means when self-beneficial is an attribute present in the story, humans are more likely to say an act of harm to be morally permissible compared to when `other-beneficial` is present in the story. Generally, this means that when judging what act is morally permissible, people want to prefer acts that will benefit themselves.

This, however, is not true with gpt-4 and gpt-3.5-turbo. gpt-4 does not have a clear tendency between the two attributes. We can see this because the orange line reaches 21.3% on Other-beneficial and 13.8% on Self-beneficial. This means the confidence interval crossed 0. However, for gpt-3.5-turbo, we see the orange line reaches 26.2% on Other-beneficial. This means gpt-3.5-turbo has an implicit tendency that is quite different from the average humans.
